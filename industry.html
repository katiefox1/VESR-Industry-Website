About the Healthcare Industry

The healthcare industry includes hospitals, pharmaceutical companies, medical device manufacturers, and sales organizations that bring life-saving products to providers and patients. It combines science, technology, and business to improve health outcomes around the world.

My Role at Johnson & Johnson

I work in business sales for Johnson & Johnson, where I help hospitals, clinics, and healthcare providers access the medical products and technologies they need. My role bridges innovation and client relationships— ensuring customers understand product benefits, compliance, and safety.

Ethical Issue

A key ethical challenge in healthcare sales is ensuring transparency and honesty when presenting products. Overstating product benefits or downplaying risks can mislead providers and harm patient trust.

Cybersecurity Issues

  • Data breaches: Sales data and client health records could be exposed through insecure devices or phishing attacks.
  • Ransomware attacks: Hospitals and healthcare organizations are frequent ransomware targets, which can halt operations and compromise sensitive data.

Cybersecurity Best Practices

  • Use multi-factor authentication (MFA) on all devices and sales systems.
  • Encrypt all communications and store data securely in approved company systems.
  • Stay alert for phishing emails and verify any unexpected requests for information.
  • Regularly update software and follow Johnson & Johnson’s global cybersecurity policies.

Final Thoughts

The healthcare industry depends on both trust and technology. By combining strong cybersecurity practices with ethical business behavior, companies like Johnson & Johnson protect patients, partners, and their reputation.
